Free features

Everything you need to know the truth.

🎯

Ping-based accuracy

Measures real-world round-trip latency to a reliable server — not signal strength bars, which can be completely misleading. Under 150ms is green. Under 500ms is yellow. Anything else is red.

🟢

Instant visual status

Green, yellow, or red — know your connection state at a glance. The pulsing dot and status text update every 2 seconds so you always have a fresh reading.

📊

Stability score

See whether your connection is Stable, Intermittent, or Poor based on jitter, packet loss, and uptime percentage. The stability card appears after your first few readings.

📈

Recent history chart

A bar chart of your last 12 readings with color-coded latency values. Tall bars = slow, short bars = fast. Instantly shows you if your connection is getting better or worse.

🧭

SignalFinder

Rotate your phone 360° to find the direction of strongest signal. Uses your compass to map latency at each bearing. Coverage %, quality score, and confidence bar show how reliable the result is.

📋

Event log

Automatically logs every outage, slowdown, and recovery with timestamps. The Events tab keeps a running tally of Outages, Slowdowns, and Recoveries so you can spot patterns.

⚡

Adjustable check rate

Check every 2, 4, 10, or 30 seconds. Use 2s when you need to know right away. Use 30s for all-day passive monitoring with minimal battery impact.

🔒

Complete privacy

TrueSignal only contacts the server you configure — nothing else. No account, no data collection, no analytics. You can even use a private local server for completely offline LAN testing.

How it works

Simple. Honest. Instant.

TrueSignal pings a reliable server every few seconds and measures the round-trip time. No algorithms, no guessing — just the truth about whether your connection can actually do anything useful right now.

● Under 150ms — Connected ● 150–500ms — Slow ● Over 500ms — No Connection

The history chart shows you the shape of your connection over time — so you can tell your ISP exactly when and how bad it gets. Tall bars mean high latency (slow). Short bars mean low latency (fast).

✦ TrueSignal Pro

Advanced tools for serious
connection diagnostics.

TrueSignal Pro unlocks a suite of powerful tools for users who need to go deeper. From $0.99/month — or $19.99 for lifetime access.

⚡

Compare Mode

Monitor up to three servers simultaneously with live latency, jitter, packet loss, and a combined verdict. Perfect for comparing your router against a public server, or testing multiple targets at once.

How to use
  1. Go to Pro → Compare
  2. Server 1 is shared with your Monitor tab
  3. Tap Change on Server 2 and 3 to add targets
  4. Watch the live verdict update at the top
  5. Green dot = healthy · Yellow = slow · Red = down
👤

Named Profiles

Save your server URL, check interval, and a name as a profile. Switch between Home, Office, Travel, or any custom setup with a single tap — no re-entering settings every time.

How to use
  1. Go to Pro → Profiles → New Profile
  2. Enter a name, server URL, and check interval
  3. Tap Save to store the profile
  4. Tap Activate on any profile to switch to it
  5. The active profile updates your Monitor server immediately
🚶

Walk Test

Record connection quality as you move through a space. The color-coded timeline shows exactly where your signal degrades — great for finding dead zones, testing router placement, or checking coverage in a building.

How to use
  1. Go to Pro → Walk Test → Start Walk
  2. Move slowly through the space you want to test
  3. Watch the timeline build in real time
  4. Tap Stop Walk, name your session, and save it
  5. Green = WiFi good · Blue = Cellular good · Yellow = slow · Red = down. Tall bars = high latency
📡

Network Connection Log

A running log of every WiFi and cellular connection change, with timestamps. Unknown WiFi networks are flagged in amber — tap to mark them as trusted or investigate further.

How to use
  1. Go to Pro → Network Log
  2. Every connection change is logged automatically from app launch
  3. Amber "Unknown" badge = a WiFi network you haven't trusted yet
  4. Tap an entry → Trust Network to mark it as known
  5. Note: WiFi network names require Android. iOS shows connection type only due to Apple's privacy restrictions
🔔

Smart Alerts

Get notified when your connection drops, slows, or recovers — but only after a sustained problem. Configurable thresholds mean you won't be spammed by brief blips. Requires TrueSignal to be open.

How to use
  1. Go to Pro → Smart Alerts
  2. Toggle Enable Smart Alerts (allows notification permission)
  3. Enable individual alerts: Connection lost, Slow connection, Connection restored
  4. Set thresholds — alert after 15s / 30s / 60s offline, or 1m / 2m / 5m slow
  5. TrueSignal must be in the foreground to fire alerts
🧭

SignalFinder — Deep Dive

SignalFinder is free and uses your phone's compass to map signal quality by direction. Rotate a full 360° for the most reliable result. The confidence bar tells you how much to trust the reading.

How to get the best results
  1. Go to the SignalFinder tab → Tap Scan
  2. Rotate your phone slowly through a full 360°
  3. Keep rotating until Confidence reaches 80%+ for best accuracy
  4. The green wedge shows the best signal direction
  5. Move towards the indicated direction and re-scan to confirm
